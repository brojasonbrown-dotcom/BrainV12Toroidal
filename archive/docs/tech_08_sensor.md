## 7. Relation to Established Technology

### 7.1 SQUID Magnetometers (Proven)

`[PROVEN TECH]` SQUIDs are the most sensitive magnetic field detectors:
- **Sensitivity:** ~10⁻¹⁵ T/√Hz (fT level).
- **Applications:** MEG (magnetoencephalography), geophysical surveys, non-destructive testing, quantum computing readout.
- **MEG:** Measures magnetic fields produced by neural currents (~10⁻¹³ T at scalp). Used for pre-surgical mapping, epilepsy localization, and cognitive neuroscience.
- **Limitations:** Require cryogenic cooling (liquid helium or cryocoolers). Sensitive to environmental noise (requiring shielded rooms).
No SQUID has ever detected a "consciousness field" or "vacuum fluctuation signature" distinct from the neural magnetic fields it is designed to measure.

### 7.2 EEG and Neuroimaging (Proven)

`[PROVEN TECH]` Standard consciousness assessment uses:
- **EEG:** 10–20 system, 19–256 channels. Measures electric potentials at scalp. Used for sleep staging, coma assessment, epilepsy monitoring, BCI.
- **fMRI:** Blood-oxygen-level-dependent (BOLD) signal. Used for functional mapping, coma prognosis, locked-in syndrome detection.
- **PET:** Metabolic activity (glucose uptake). Used for dementia diagnosis, coma assessment.
- **fNIRS:** Near-infrared spectroscopy. Measures hemodynamic changes. Portable alternative to fMRI.
None of these modalities detect "consciousness fields" in the vacuum. They measure neural activity correlates of consciousness (NCC), not consciousness itself as a field.

### 7.3 Quantum Sensors (Emerging)

`[EMERGING]` Advanced quantum sensors include:
- **Optomechanical sensors:** Use optical cavities to measure mechanical displacement at quantum limit. Sensitivity ~10⁻²⁰ m/√Hz.
- **NV-center magnetometers:** Nitrogen-vacancy centers in diamond detect magnetic fields at room temperature. Sensitivity ~nT/√Hz (less than SQUIDs but portable).
- **Atomic clocks:** Optical lattice clocks achieve stability of 10⁻¹⁸. Used for timekeeping, geodesy, and fundamental physics tests.
- **Gravitational wave detectors:** LIGO uses interferometry to detect strain ~10⁻²³/√Hz. Measures spacetime metric perturbations, not vacuum fluctuations or consciousness.
None of these quantum sensors have detected the phenomena claimed by the RHSA (consciousness fields, vacuum fluctuation spikes, entanglement bridges).

### 7.4 Sensor Array Design (Established)

`[EST PHYS]` Sensor array design is a mature field:
- **Beamforming:** Phased arrays for directional sensitivity (radio astronomy, sonar, radar).
- **Compressive sensing:** Sparse sampling for signal reconstruction.
- **Optimal array geometry:** Depends on the spatial frequency content of the signal. Common geometries: uniform linear, circular, random, hexagonal.
No optimal array design theory uses the Metatron lattice or φ-scaled spacing. The 13-node Metatron configuration is not an optimal array for any known signal detection problem.
