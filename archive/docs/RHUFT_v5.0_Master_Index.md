## Critical Research Findings

### 1. The Fine-Structure Constant Formula Is Mathematically Wrong
The RHUFT formula α⁻¹ = X_φ + φ⁻¹⁰/2 where X_φ = 360°/arctan(1/φ) evaluates to **11.35**, not 137.036. The claimed 99.9999999% accuracy is **false**. Three independent formulas in the text give ~11.35, ~11.36, and ~1.76 — all wildly incorrect. This is the single most devastating finding, as it undermines the central claim of the framework.

### 2. The Base Frequency f₀ Is Off by 24 Orders of Magnitude
The formula f₀ = c/(ℓ_P·φ⁷·√π) yields **3.6 × 10⁴¹ Hz**, not 9.01 × 10¹⁷ Hz. All five "convergence methods" also produce ~10⁴⁰–10⁴¹ Hz. The value 9.01 × 10¹⁷ Hz is an unexplained postulate, not a derived constant.

### 3. The Master Equation Is Dimensionally Inconsistent
Term III (κ ∮ ∇Ψ · dS) has units `[Ψ]·[L]²` while Terms I and II have units `[Ψ]`. A physically valid equation cannot add terms with different dimensions. Three corrected forms are proposed in the refined Chapter 4.

### 4. The Scale Hierarchy Fails at Every Level
The formula L_n = ℓ_P·φ^{10n}·δ_n/φ gives 10⁻³⁵–10⁻²⁵ m, not 10⁻¹⁵–10¹⁰ m. Every claimed decade is wrong by 20–35 orders of magnitude.

### 5. The Proton Radius Formula Is Off by 9–24 Orders
The formula r_p = ℓ_P·φ⁵⁵ gives ~5 × 10⁻²⁴ m, not 0.84 fm. The exponent would need to be ~96, not 55. The proton radius puzzle is now resolved toward ~0.8414 fm (muonic hydrogen value), contradicting RHUFT's dual-radius claim.

### 6. No Peer-Reviewed Physics Supports the Metatron Lattice
The 13-node Metatron lattice, φ-scaling at Planck scale, and Silver Ratio as a physics constant appear in **zero** peer-reviewed quantum gravity papers (LQG, CDT, string theory, asymptotic safety, causal sets). These elements are numerological assignments, not established physics.

### 7. Post-Hoc Fitting, Not Prediction
RHUFT consistently takes known measured values, constructs formulas with tunable exponents, and claims the results were "derived from first principles." This is **retrodictive fitting**, not predictive science. A genuine theory must predict unknown quantities.

### 8. Internal Inconsistencies Across the Corpus
- Different chapters use different mass formulas (ℏ vs h, factor of 2π difference)
- Different node assignments for the same particles (electron at n=1 in one place, n=11 in another)
- The top quark mass is not addressed by the formula (predicts ~10 GeV, actual ~173 GeV)
- Neutrino masses are entirely omitted
- The CMB peak ℓ ≈ 220 is claimed to equal φ⁶ ≈ 17.9 — a straightforward numerical error
