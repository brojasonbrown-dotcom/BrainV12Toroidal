## Abstract

The universe contains matter but almost no antimatter, with a baryon-to-photon ratio $\eta_B \approx 6.1 \times 10^{-10}$. The Standard Model explains this via the Sakharov conditions (baryon number violation, C and CP violation, and departure from thermal equilibrium), but cannot quantitatively predict the observed asymmetry because CP violation in the SM is too small. RHUFT claims to derive $\eta_B$ from geometric recursion. This refined document evaluates the RHUFT derivation, identifies its post-hoc fitting nature, and situates the discussion within the established physics of baryogenesis.

## 1. The Observed Baryon Asymmetry

[OBSERVED] The baryon-to-photon ratio is measured from:
- **CMB anisotropies** (Planck 2018): $\eta_B = 6.12 \times 10^{-10}$ with uncertainty ~0.5%
- **Big Bang nucleosynthesis** (light element abundances): $\eta_B = 5.8$–$6.6 \times 10^{-10}$
- **Lyman-α forest** and galaxy cluster gas fractions (independent, consistent values)

[OBSERVED] The universe contains no detectable primordial antimatter. The nearest antigalaxies, if they exist, would produce annihilation gamma rays at the boundaries; no such signal is observed. The baryon asymmetry is a **global** feature of the observable universe.

## 2. The Sakharov Conditions: Established Physics

[EST PHYS] In 1967, Andrei Sakharov identified three necessary conditions for baryogenesis:

1. **Baryon number violation**: Required to produce a net baryon number from an initially symmetric state. The SM has baryon number violation via electroweak sphalerons (non-perturbative processes in SU(2)$_L$ with rate $\Gamma_{\text{sph}} \sim \exp(-4\pi / \alpha_W) \sim 10^{-160}$ at zero temperature, but significant at $T \gtrsim 100$ GeV).

2. **C and CP violation**: Required so that baryon-producing and antibaryon-producing processes proceed at different rates. The SM has CP violation in the CKM matrix (quark mixing) and the PMNS matrix (neutrino mixing), but the CKM phase is too small to explain the observed asymmetry by many orders of magnitude.

3. **Departure from thermal equilibrium**: Required so that inverse processes do not erase the asymmetry. In the SM, the electroweak phase transition is a smooth crossover (not first-order), so this condition is not satisfied in the minimal SM. Extensions (e.g., two-Higgs-doublet models, singlet extensions) can provide a first-order phase transition.

[EST PHYS] The SM cannot explain the observed baryon asymmetry. The CP violation in the CKM matrix yields $\eta_B \sim 10^{-20}$, far below the observed $6 \times 10^{-10}$. This is one of the strongest motivations for **Beyond the Standard Model (BSM) physics**.

## 3. Baryogenesis Mechanisms: The Frontiers

[SPEC] Several BSM mechanisms are actively studied but none are established:

### 3.1 Electroweak Baryogenesis
If the electroweak phase transition were first-order (possible in some extended Higgs sectors), bubble walls during the transition could provide out-of-equilibrium conditions. CP violation from additional Higgs phases or right-handed neutrinos could bias baryon production over antibaryon production. This mechanism is testable at colliders (search for extended Higgs sectors) but is disfavored by current LHC data.

### 3.2 Leptogenesis
[SPEC] Perhaps the most promising mechanism. The seesaw mechanism for neutrino masses introduces heavy right-handed neutrinos. These can decay out of equilibrium in the early universe, producing a lepton asymmetry that is partially converted to baryon asymmetry by sphalerons. This requires:
- Heavy neutrino masses $M_N \sim 10^9$–$10^{12}$ GeV
- CP-violating phases in the neutrino Yukawa couplings
- A viable neutrino mass model (type I, II, or III seesaw)

This mechanism is natural but not directly testable at current energies.

### 3.3 Affleck-Dine Baryogenesis
In supersymmetric models, scalar field condensates (e.g., squark/slepton fields) can carry baryon number. Their coherent evolution along flat directions in the potential can generate large baryon asymmetries. This requires supersymmetry, which has not been observed at the LHC.

### 3.4 GUT Baryogenesis
Grand Unified Theories (GUTs) unify the strong, weak, and electromagnetic forces at $E \sim 10^{16}$ GeV. GUT gauge bosons (X, Y) can decay with baryon-number-violating branching ratios. This was historically the first proposed mechanism but is now disfavored because GUT-scale baryogenesis would be erased by electroweak sphalerons at $T \sim 100$ GeV unless accompanied by B-L violation.

## 4. RHUFT Claims: Evaluation

### 4.1 The Geometric Derivation

[SPEC] RHUFT claims that the baryon asymmetry emerges from the "failure rate of recursive cancellation" in the vacuum field. The derivation introduces several post-hoc ingredients:

- The coherence asymmetry $\Delta C = C_B - C_{\bar{B}}$ is defined but not connected to any physical quantity (baryon number, lepton number, etc.).
- The factor $2\pi / \phi^{10} \approx 0.0511$ is chosen because it is a small number of the right order of magnitude.
- The combination $\eta_B = (\lambda \cdot \kappa / \phi^{10}) \times (n_{\text{fluctuations}} / n_\gamma)$ is constructed to yield $\sim 6 \times 10^{-10}$ by choosing the ratio $n_{\text{fluctuations}} / n_\gamma$ appropriately.

[NUMERO] This is **post-hoc fitting**, not prediction. The RHUFT formula has the schematic form:

$$\eta_B = \text{(small number)} \times \text{(another small number)} \times \text{(large number)}$$

with each factor chosen after the fact to match the known value. The claim that this is a "prediction, not a postdiction" is false.

### 4.2 The CP Violation Claim

[DISPROV] RHUFT claims that the CP-violating phase is derived as:

$$\delta_{CP} = \frac{\pi}{\phi^5} \approx 0.719 \text{ rad} \approx 41.2°$$

and that this is "remarkably consistent with observed CP violation in $B_d^0$ mixing ($\sin 2\beta \approx 0.68$)."

[OBSERVED] The CP violation parameter in the CKM matrix is the angle $\beta$ (or $\phi_1$), with $\sin 2\beta = 0.699 \pm 0.017$ (PDG 2024). The RHUFT prediction of $\delta_{CP} \approx 41.2°$ is not the same as the CKM phase. The CKM phase is $\delta_{CKM} \approx 68°$ (the unitarity triangle angle). The neutrino CP-violating phase $\delta_{CP}^{\text{PMNS}}$ is currently measured to be around $195°$–$234°$ (depending on mass hierarchy), not $41.2°$.

[SPEC] Moreover, the RHUFT formula $\delta_{CP} = \pi / \phi^5$ is arbitrary. There is no physical principle linking CP violation to the golden ratio. The baryon asymmetry requires CP violation in the early universe, not necessarily the same phase as appears in $B_d^0$ mixing. The SM electroweak CP violation is known to be too small to explain the observed asymmetry; the RHUFT formula does not explain why the SM fails or how BSM physics would correct it.

### 4.3 The Sakharov Conditions in RHUFT

[SPEC] RHUFT presents a comparison table claiming to subsume Sakharov's conditions into a "unified geometric framework." However:
- **Baryon number violation**: RHUFT describes it as "knot formation at Metatron boundary." This is not equivalent to electroweak sphalerons or GUT-scale baryon violation. It provides no rate, no temperature dependence, and no cross-section.
- **CP violation**: RHUFT claims it is "intrinsic to temporal feedback." This is not equivalent to the CKM or PMNS complex phases. It provides no Lagrangian, no Feynman rules, and no calculation of CP-violating observables.
- **Departure from equilibrium**: RHUFT claims it is "coherence threshold crossing." This is not equivalent to a first-order phase transition or out-of-equilibrium decay. It provides no free energy barrier, no bubble nucleation rate, and no transport equations.

[SPEC] RHUFT does not reproduce the Standard Model's baryon-number-violating processes, its CP-violating observables, or its phase transition dynamics. It replaces established physics with geometric metaphors that are not mathematically equivalent.

## 6. Relation to Established Physics: The Baryogenesis Frontier

The baryon asymmetry is one of the most important unsolved problems in particle physics and cosmology. Unlike the RHUFT approach, which claims to have solved it via geometric numerology, the physics community is actively pursuing multiple avenues:

- **Neutrino experiments** (JUNO, DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande) will measure the CP-violating phase $\delta_{CP}$ in the PMNS matrix, testing whether leptogenesis is viable.
- **Collider searches** (LHC, HL-LHC) probe extended Higgs sectors that could enable electroweak baryogenesis.
- **Gravitational wave detectors** (LISA, Einstein Telescope) may detect first-order phase transitions in the early universe, providing direct evidence for baryogenesis mechanisms.
- **Precision CMB measurements** constrain the baryon density and any exotic baryon-number-violating processes.

RHUFT contributes none of these testable predictions. Its "geometric derivation" of the baryon asymmetry is a closed circle: it takes the known value $\eta_B \approx 6.1 \times 10^{-10}$, constructs a product of small factors involving $\phi$, $\lambda$, and $\kappa$, and declares the result a prediction. This is not how physics works.
