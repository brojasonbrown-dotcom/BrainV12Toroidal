- **Monopole problem:** Grand unified theories predict magnetic monopoles that should dominate the universe's mass density; none are observed. [EST PHYS]

[EST PHYS] Inflation solves these by positing a brief epoch of accelerated exponential expansion driven by a scalar field (the inflaton) with a nearly constant potential energy density. [EST PHYS] Regions in causal contact before inflation are stretched beyond the current horizon, explaining the uniformity of the CMB. [EST PHYS] The exponential expansion drives Ω_k → 0, solving the flatness problem. [EST PHYS] Monopoles are diluted to unobservable density. [EST PHYS]
