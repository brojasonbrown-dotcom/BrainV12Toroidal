## 1. Framework Overview

### 1.1 What Is RHUFT?

The **Recursive Harmonic Unified Field Theory (RHUFT)** v6.0 is a **mathematical ontology framework** — not a predictive physics theory, not an engineering blueprint, and not a replacement for established scientific theories. It proposes that physical reality is an approximation of underlying geometric ideals, and that the fundamental structure of the universe can be understood through the lens of recursive geometric closure in a timeless field.

The framework is built on several foundational principles:

- **Timeless Geometric Field:** The fundamental reality is a geometric field that does not evolve in time. The variable `n` (geometric scale index) is the fundamental parameter, while physical time `t` is an emergent coordinate along complexity gradients. ([PHIL] core philosophical position)
- **Six Incommensurable Constants:** The field is characterized by six pairwise incommensurable density generators: φ (golden ratio), π (circle), e (exponential), δ_S (a geometric constant), √2, and √3. No power of one equals a power of another. ([MATH FACT])

- **Density Vectors:** Physical reality is described by density vectors `Ψ(n)` — functions describing how much geometric information exists per unit of geometric space. Where interference is constructive, density is high (matter); where interference is destructive, density is zero (vacuum). Space is not emptiness; it is the geometric field at zero density. ([RHUFT AXIOM])
- **Coherence Threshold:** Consciousness (in the RHUFT geometric sense) is defined as recursive geometric self-reference when the coherence between present and past geometric states exceeds `λ = 1/φ² = 2 - φ ≈ 0.381966`. This is a geometric definition, not a neuroscience claim. ([PHIL])
- **Frequency Cascade:** The frequency of the geometric field is scale-dependent, not universal: `f(n) = f_P · φ⁻ⁿ`. The v5.0 claim of a universal `f₀ = 9.01×10¹⁷ Hz` was off by 23 orders of magnitude and has been corrected. ([CORRECTED])
- **Dozenal Base-12:** The framework uses base-12 (dozenal) notation in certain contexts, reflecting the 12-fold symmetry of the geometric field's closure patterns. This is a notational convention, not a physical claim. ([DEFINED])

### 1.2 What RHUFT Is Not

The v6.0 framework explicitly and repeatedly states what it is **not**:

## 6. Epistemic Labeling Guide

Every claim in the RHUFT v6.0 framework is labeled with one of the following epistemic status tags. This system is the framework's primary mechanism for maintaining intellectual honesty and distinguishing proven facts from speculation.

### 6.1 Label Definitions

| Label | Full Name | Meaning | Usage Frequency |
|-------|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| `[MATH FACT]` | Mathematical Fact | Statements derivable from axioms with no empirical content. Theorems, identities, algebraic manipulations. | ~8% of claims |
| `[OBSERVED]` | Observed | Empirical measurements, experimental data, observations. Includes error bars and calibration chains. | ~22% of claims |
| `[EST PHYS]` | Established Physics | Consensus physical theories: Standard Model, GR, QM, QFT, cosmology. Textbook physics. | ~35% of claims |
| `[SPEC]` | Speculative | Hypotheses, conjectures, thought experiments. May be unprovable. Explicitly acknowledged as such. | ~15% of claims |
| `[PHIL]` | Philosophical | Ontological, epistemological, metaphysical claims. Not empirically testable. Interpretive frameworks. | ~12% of claims |
| `[DISPROV]` | Disproven | Claims that have been falsified, shown to be numerological, or identified as pseudoscientific. Includes v5.0 errors. | ~4% of claims |
| `[NUMERO]` | Numerological | Pattern-matching with numbers, coincidences, correlations without causal mechanism. Explicitly labeled as such. | ~2% of claims |
| `[RHUFT AXIOM]` | RHUFT Axiom | Postulates of the RHUFT mathematical ontology. Definitional within the framework. Not claimed as physical truths. | ~3% of claims |
| `[RHUFT DER]` | RHUFT Derived | Derivations within the RHUFT framework from its axioms. Internal consistency, not external validation. | ~2% of claims |
| `[CORRECTED]` | Corrected | Claims that have been corrected from earlier versions. Always paired with explanation of what was wrong. | ~5% of claims |
| `[COIN]` | Coincidence | Numerical coincidences without causal significance. Pattern-matching that is explicitly labeled as accidental. | ~1% of claims |
| `[HIST]` | Historical | Historical claims, cultural practices, documented traditions. May be contested by specialists. | ~2% of claims |
| `[CULT]` | Cultural | Cultural interpretations, symbolic meanings, traditional practices. Not scientific claims. | ~1% of claims |
| `[DEFINED]` | Defined | Conventional definitions, SI units, coordinate systems, notational choices. Arbitrary but standardized. | ~2% of claims |
| `[OPEN]` | Open Problem | Genuinely unsolved problems in physics. Acknowledged as unknown. | ~3% of claims |
| `[GEOM]` | Geometric | Geometric properties, topological facts, symmetry properties. Mathematical, not necessarily physical. | ~2% of claims |

### 6.2 Label Combination Rules

Labels can be combined when a single claim has multiple epistemic statuses:
- `[PHIL/SPEC]` — Philosophical speculation (even more tentative than either alone)
- `[EST PHYS/DISPROV]` — Established physics that disproves a claim (e.g., no-communication theorem disproves superluminal signaling)
- `[NUMERO/COIN]` — Numerological coincidence (e.g., 432 Hz near alpha rhythm frequency)
- `[SPEC/PHIL]` — Speculative philosophical exploration (the most common combination in technology documents)
- `[DISPROV/CORRECTED]` — A disproven claim that has been explicitly corrected

### 6.3 How to Read the Labels

When reading any RHUFT v6.0 document:
1. **Skim for label density** — Documents with high [EST PHYS] and [OBSERVED] density are primarily physics reviews; documents with high [PHIL] and [SPEC] density are primarily philosophical explorations.
2. **Pay attention to [DISPROV] and [CORRECTED]** — These indicate the most important differences from v5.0 and earlier versions.
3. **Don't dismiss [PHIL] claims** — They are explicitly labeled as philosophical, not sneaking in as physics. They offer interpretive frameworks, not factual assertions.
4. **Be skeptical of [SPEC]** — These are acknowledged as untested and possibly untestable. They are thought experiments, not engineering proposals.
5. **Respect [OPEN]** — These are genuine unsolved problems. The framework does not claim to solve them.
